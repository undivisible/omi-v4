The hardware

Two and a half centimetres of listening.

Omi is a 2.5cm disc, 1.5cm deep, on a lanyard or a wrist band. It records what you say and hear, streams it to your phone over Bluetooth LE 5.1, and keeps recording when the phone is out of range — the audio catches up when it comes back.

  • Size2.5cm diameter, 1.5cm deep
  • Battery150 mAh, 10–14 hours
  • RadioBluetooth 5.1; Wi-Fi 2.4/5 GHz
  • Latency500–2000 ms live; 10–20 s offline
  • Offline recordingYes — it catches up when the phone is back
  • ChargingDock with pogo-pin contacts
  • Languages25+, single, multi, or translated
  • In transitTLS
  • At restAES-256-GCM
  • Training on your dataNo
  • CompatibilityiOS 15+, Android 7+, macOS, any browser
  • Water resistanceNone — keep it out of the shower

Open surface

POST/mcp

A public HTTP API and an MCP server, so the tools you already use can ask your second brain too.

  • The same boundary as the app. Every request carries your credential; every row is scoped to your account before it is read.
  • OpenAI-compatible chat. /v1/chat/completions streams in the shape your clients already speak.

Privacy

Your memory lives on your machine.

  • Local source of truth. The cloud keeps only a rebuildable projection for recall.
  • On-device summaries. The workspace scan reads metadata, and on Apple silicon it summarises with no network call.
  • Open source. The line between local and cloud is something you can read.

Negotiate

Haggle with Omi. It is not a metaphor.

Bring your own key and the price is not a plan you pick, it is a conversation you have. Omi opens a session, you argue your case, and what you agree is what you are charged — because the agreement is enforced on the server, not in the app.

  • The model never sets the price. It may suggest at most one concession per reply, from a closed list the server sent it. The server turns codes into money.
  • There is a floor. Grants are de-duplicated, subtracted from the standard price, and clamped. No combination — forged or replayed — lands below it.
  • The prose cannot lie. Any figure in a reply is rewritten to the figure the server computed before you ever see it.
  • Accepting recomputes. Checkout reads the agreed price server-side; no caller passes one in. The transcript is kept with the outcome.
  • Skipping is a real path. Take the standard price and it is recorded like any other outcome.
